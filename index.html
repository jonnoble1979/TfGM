Greater Manchester Road Safety Audit (RSA) Procedure

These updated procedure have been developed for all Greater Manchester Partners to ensure that RSAs are carried out in a consistent and systematic way within Greater Manchester and sets out the key principles for undertaking RSAs.
They should apply to the Greater Manchester road network and for all other improvement schemes undertaken on Metrolink network and GM Bee Network Interchanges.

Getting Advice on Road Safety

All design projects should undergo a Road Safety Review early in the design. The cost for this is minimal (1-2 hours of an expert's time), and ensures designers are aware of road safety issues early. This minimises potential issues and re-design. Contact the WSP road safety team to arrange this. They will ensure the team doing the reviews is different from the one doing the road safety audits to ensure the total independence of the latter from the design as per process.

At any time during the design process if you have any road safety concerns with your projects the WSP road safety team are here and available to offer an opinion, help and advice (again they will make sure independence of the RSA team is maintained).

Time required for a safety review could be as little as 1 hour.

2. Applicability of RSA

Where there are physical changes to the highway impacting on road user behaviour or resulting in a change to the outcome of a collision on the trunk road and motorway network, road safety audit (RSA) shall apply, regardless of the procurement method.

Temporary traffic management and temporary changes to the highway not associated with the construction of a highway scheme, and that last longer than 6 months in duration, are considered to be physical changes to the highway.

RSA shall not apply where a physical change to the highway will not impact on road user behaviour, or change the outcome of a collision on the trunk road and motorway network.

The Overseeing Organisation shall produce an exemption file note to be kept on the scheme file (or equivalent) where there is no need to apply RSA on the trunk road and motorway network.

An exemption file note template is provided in appendix A.

6. Response report

A RSA response report shall be produced for stage 1, 2 and 3 RSAs. NOTE A RSA response report is not be required for stage 4 RSAs.

  • The design organisation shall manage the production of the RSA response report in collaboration with the Overseeing Organisation.
  • The RSA response report shall include a summary of the scheme, the stage of RSA, the RSA report document reference and date of the RSA report it relates to.
  • The RSA response report shall contain details of the representatives from the design organisation who prepared the RSA response report.
  • The RSA response report shall contain a RSA decision log to include a reiteration of each road safety problem and recommendation made in the RSA report.
  • For each road safety problem and recommendation, the design organisation shall do one of the following:
    1. accept the road safety problem and recommendation made by the RSA team;
    2. accept the road safety problem raised, but suggest an alternative solution, giving appropriate reasoning; or
    3. disagree with the road safety problem and recommendation raised, giving appropriate reasoning for rejecting both.
  • The RSA response report shall contain a response from the Overseeing Organisation and a RSA action for each problem agreed between the design organisation and Overseeing Organisation.
  • The RSA response report shall be signed by the Overseeing Organisation and design organisation to indicate their agreement on the RSA actions.
  • The RSA response report should be produced and finalised within one month of the issue of the RSA report.
  • For each RSA action, either the design organisation or Overseeing Organisation shall be responsible for its implementation.
