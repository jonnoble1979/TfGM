Road Safety Audit reports

RSA Report

All reports should follow the template report layout, which can be downloaded above

The RSA report shall be structured logically within headings and include the following details:

  • RSA stage
  • Description of the proposed Improvement Scheme including location and its objectives.
  • Details of RSA brief originator, approver of the brief and the RSA Team.
  • RSA Team membership as well as the names of others contributing such as the RSA Team Observer, Police, Maintaining Agent and Specialist Advisors.
  • Who was present at the site visit, the date and time period(s) when it was undertaken and what the site conditions were on the day of the visit (weather, traffic congestion, etc.).
  • The specific road safety problems identified supported with the background reasoning.
  • Recommendations for action to mitigate or remove the road safety problems.
  • A location map based on the scheme plan(s), marked up and referenced to problems and if available, photographs of the problems identified.
  • A statement, signed by both the RSA Team Leader and the RSA Team Member(s).
  • A list of documents and drawings reviewed for the RSA.

An illustrative Stage 2 RSA Report can be downloaded above.

The RSA Team shall send a copy of the RSA Report directly to the originator of the brief, a copy to the Scheme Manager and a copy to TfGM at roadsafetyaudit@tfgm.com.

The RSA Team Leader should send any comments on matters that are not covered by the RSA brief to the Scheme Manager in separate correspondence.

Maintenance defects noted during site visits shall be immediately reported by the Project Sponsor.

On receipt of the finalised RSA Report, the Scheme Manager shall issue the document to the Design Team to allow them to input into a joint RSA Response Report in accordance with this procedure.

Response Report

An RSA response report shall be produced for Stage 1, 2 and 3 RSAs (including any Interim Stages of Audit). An RSA response report is not required for Stage 4 RSAs.

The design organisation shall manage the production of the RSA response report in collaboration with the Overseeing Organisation.

The RSA response report shall include a summary of the scheme, the stage of RSA, the RSA report document reference and date of the RSA report to which it relates.

The RSA response report shall contain details of the representatives from the design organisation who prepared the RSA response report.

The RSA response report shall contain an RSA decision log to include a reiteration of each road safety problem and recommendation made in the RSA report.

The design organisation shall, for each RSA problem and recommendation, do one of the following:

  1. Accept the RSA problem and recommendation made by the RSA team;
  2. Accept the RSA problem raised, but suggest an alternative solution, giving appropriate reasoning;
  3. Disagree with the RSA problem and recommendation raised, giving appropriate reasoning for rejecting both.

The RSA response report shall contain a response from the Overseeing Organisation and an RSA action for each problem agreed between the design organisation and Overseeing Organisation.

The RSA response report shall be signed by the Overseeing Organisation and design organisation to indicate their agreement on the RSA actions. The RSA response report should be produced and finalised within one month of the issue of the RSA report.

The RSA response report should be produced and finalised within one month of the issue of the RSA report.

An example RSA response report template for Stages 1, 2 and 3 can be downloaded above.

For each RSA action, either the design organisation or Overseeing Organisation shall be responsible for its implementation.
