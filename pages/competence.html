Team Approval

The Scheme Manager should select an RSA Team that comprises at least two competent people with appropriate skills; knowledge; experience; and qualifications to the satisfaction of the Overseeing Organisation whether that is the Highway Authority or TfGM for TfGM projects.

When assessing the competency of an RSA Team, details of both the RSA Team Leader and the RSA Team Member are shall be required in all instances.

Approvals of the RSA team are scheme and RSA stage-specific and the use of personnel or organisations on previous RSAs should not guarantee their suitability to undertake an RSA on other schemes.

Proposed members of the RSA team shall demonstrate their competency by means of a road safety specific curriculum vitae (CV) detailing training, continuing professional development (CPD) and experience.

Experience should be relevant to the type of scheme being subject to RSA and identified in the proposed RSA team members' CV.

Where an RSA is requested on schemes which feature proposals in the vicinity of any Metrolink or transport Interchange infrastructure at least one member of the RSA Team should be able to demonstrate prior experience (e.g. scheme design, collision investigation or Road Safety Auditing) of these types of schemes. The suitability of the RSA Team’s experience will be assessed by the Scheme Manager on a scheme-by-scheme basis.

Qualifications and Experience

The recommended minimum qualifications and experience for RSA Team Leaders and Team Members is as follows:

  • Attendance of a 10-day formally structured and recognised Collision Investigation or Road Safety Engineering course. A condensed course following the same syllabus is deemed satisfactory to meet this requirement.
  • A minimum of 4 years Collision Investigation or Road Safety Engineering experience (RSA Team Leader), or a minimum of 1 years Collision Investigation or Road Safety Engineering experience (RSA Team Member).
  • Completion of at least 5 RSAs in the past 12 months (RSA Team Leader), or completion of at least 5 RSAs in the past 24 months (RSA Team Member).
  • A minimum of 2 days certificated evidenced Continued Professional Development (CPD) in the fields of RSA, Collision Investigation or Road Safety Engineering in the past 12 months.

On occasions an individual with the appropriate training, skills and experience may accompany the RSA Team as an Observer in order for them to gain experience of the RSA process. The RSA Team Observer is encouraged to contribute actively to the RSA process.

An RSA Observer shall have acceptable levels of training, skills and experience prior to accompanying the RSA Team in order that they gain worthwhile experience from the RSA.

As a minimum, an RSA Team Observer shall have undertaken a 2-day formally structured and recognised Collision Investigation or Road Safety Engineering course.

There is no minimum requirement for RSA Observers with regards to Collision Investigation or Road Safety Engineering experience.

Others Involved

Police Representative

A representative from Greater Manchester Police should be invited to all Stage 3 RSAs and can be invited to Stage 1 and 2 RSAs at the discretion of the Scheme Manager. It will be at Greater Manchester Police’s discretion as to whether they attend.

Specialists

Where required by the RSA team, specialist advisors shall be approved by the Overseeing Organisation.

A specialist advisor shall be independent of the RSA team and highway scheme conception, design, construction and operation.

Maintaining Agent

A representative from the maintaining agent should be invited to all Stage 3 RSAs and can be invited to Stage 1 and 2 RSAs at the discretion of the Scheme Manager.

The Police, Specialist Advisors, Maintaining Agent and RSA Team Observers do not form part of the formal RSA Team, but assist the RSA Team in the completion of the RSA.
